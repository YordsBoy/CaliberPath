description: >
  Possesses fundamental computer and internet skills needed in most modern jobs; can operate a computer or smart device; understands basic cybersecurity practices (strong passwords, recognizing phishing) and can learn new software or systems with minimal guidance. Confidently uses digital tools, evaluates online information, and applies technology to solve problems and communicate effectively.
source_frameworks:
  - "Derived from EU DigComp 2.2 framework (publicly released, EC JRC); adapted from O*NET Knowledge 'Computers and Electronics' (USDOL/ETA, CC BY 4.0)"
proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: Has seen or used a computer/phone for simple tasks (social media, typing a short document); knows basic terminology (file, browser, login); performs basic actions (email, word processing); navigates the internet safely with guidance.
  - level: Basic
    indicator: Performs essential digital tasks (i.e., uses documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and collaboration platforms) independently; manages file storage, retrieval, and sharing securely; writes emails, fills online forms, follows security basics (doesn’t share passwords, runs antivirus if prompted).
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: Adopts new workplace software and platforms independently, including AI-assisted tools for drafting, research, or data analysis; uses collaborative tools (shared drives, video meetings) effectively; troubleshoots minor technical issues and escalates larger problems appropriately; applies cybersecurity practices (recognizing phishing attempts, enabling multi-factor authentication).
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: Integrates multiple digital tools — including AI productivity assistants and workflow automation platforms — to streamline processes and reduce manual effort; customizes tool settings for efficiency and accessibility; serves as digital resource and troubleshooter for teammates; efficiently manages complex data (organizing files, applying spreadsheet formulas, using database queries); proactively updates digital skills in response to emerging technologies.
  - level: Expert
    indicator: Leads digital adoption initiatives; ensures digital-inclusion best practices; mentors others in advanced tool optimization and cybersecurity hygiene.
