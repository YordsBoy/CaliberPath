## How to Use This Document

This document organizes career transition pathways by transferable competency domain rather than by the client's current sector — because most career changers identify more readily with what they are good at than with the industry they happen to be in. Start by identifying the client's 2–3 strongest transferable domains from their Career Compass intake or UPLS assessment results. Navigate to those domain profiles below and review the Transfer Pathways table to see which destination sectors have the strongest adjacency to that domain. Use the Typical Transition Profile as a calibration check — if the description matches the client's background, the pathway data is applicable. Where a client carries strength in two domains, review both and look for sectors that appear in both tables; those are the highest-confidence destinations.

### Domain 1: Safety & Compliance

**Cluster Tags in This Domain:** Safety · Compliance · Regulatory Affairs · Risk Management · OSHA 1910 · OSHA 1926 · Environmental Health & Safety · Hazmat · CBRNE

**Richest Sectors by Tag Presence:**
Manufacturing · Transportation & Logistics · Construction & Infrastructure · Mining & Extraction · Energy & Utilities · Agriculture & Natural Resources · Personal & Other Services · Maker Economy & Creative Crafts

**Transfer Pathways:**

| From Sector(s) | To Sector(s) | Adjacency Score | Entry Point |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manufacturing | Transportation & Logistics | HIGH (7 shared) | Safety Coordinator, Fleet Safety Manager, Logistics Compliance Specialist |
| Transportation & Logistics | Construction & Infrastructure | MEDIUM (6 shared) | Site Safety Officer, Construction Compliance Manager |
| Manufacturing | Mining & Extraction | MEDIUM (6 shared) | Mine Safety Inspector, EHS Manager, Operations Supervisor |
| Construction | Manufacturing | MEDIUM (6 shared) | Production Safety Manager, Quality & Safety Coordinator |
| Energy & Utilities | Mining & Extraction | MEDIUM (4 shared) | Environmental Health & Safety Specialist, Regulatory Affairs Analyst |
| Any Industrial | Government & Public Administration | LOW (2–3 shared) | OSHA Compliance Officer, Regulatory Inspector, Safety Program Manager |

**Typical Transition Profile:** This domain describes someone who has spent 5–15 years in an industrial or operational environment — manufacturing floor supervisor, construction foreman, logistics operations manager, military logistics or safety NCO — and whose deepest competency is not the technical process but the ability to keep people safe, maintain compliance, and manage operational risk. They often have OSHA certifications, incident investigation experience, and documentation habits that civilian employers value highly but that may not be captured in a job title.

**Entry Roles in Destination Sectors:**
- Environmental Health & Safety (EHS) Specialist or Manager — broad applicability across all industrial sectors; CSRA employers include Kimberly-Clark, Club Car, and Fort Gordon civilian workforce
- Safety Coordinator / Safety Officer — entry-level; accessible without graduate credentials
- Compliance Analyst / Regulatory Affairs Specialist — requires more industry-specific knowledge but values the operational credibility of someone who has actually worked in the environment they are regulating
- Quality Assurance / Quality Control Manager — adjacent domain; compliance mindset transfers directly

**CaliberPath Service Match:** Career Compass sessions 1–3 should inventory certifications (OSHA 10/30, CHST, ASP, CSP) and map them against target sector job postings. Many clients in this domain are technically over-qualified for entry-level EHS roles and under-credentialed for senior roles — identifying the right salary band and credential investment is the primary coaching task.

Clients in this domain have spent their careers moving things, managing inventory, maintaining equipment, coordinating schedules, and ensuring operational continuity. Sessions 2–4 should produce a concrete credential plan with timeline and cost. CSRA employer mapping is high-value — this population has strong local market options.

A burned-out ER nurse and a nurse practitioner wanting to move into health tech consulting have very different roadmaps despite the same starting sector. Stream 4 assessment should prioritize UPLS Well-being and Resilience competencies alongside the professional domain competencies.

## CSRA-Specific Pathway Notes

The Augusta-Richmond County MSA shapes which of these domain pathways have the strongest local market pull. The following notes help coaches calibrate national adjacency data against CSRA conditions:

**Highest local demand domains:** Safety & Compliance (Kimberly-Clark, Club Car, Fort Gordon industrial base) · Digital Technology & Cloud (ARCYBER contractor corridor, Georgia Cyber Center) · Operations & Logistics (CSRA manufacturing and distribution employers) · Community & Social Services (large nonprofit sector anchored by AU Health system and veteran services organizations)

**Underserved local supply:** Data & Analytics professionals are in shorter supply locally than nationally, creating a favorable candidate market for clients in this domain willing to stay in CSRA. Finance & Business Management professionals are well-supplied from the Augusta University business program pipeline, creating a more competitive market for clients entering without credentials.

**Watch_2030 local opportunities:** Sustainability & Environment (solar installation activity in South Carolina and Georgia corridor) · Immersive Media / Creative (Fort Gordon's emerging immersive training programs and the nascent Cyber District creative ecosystem) · Healthcare & Human Services (AU Health system expansion and the aging CSRA population creating sustained demand; the Fort Gordon military medic pipeline — 68W Combat Medic Specialists and Navy/Marine corpsmen — represents a sustained, clinically experienced transition population that is underrecognized by most CSRA coaching providers; CaliberPath's OI-09 KSA expansion and HC-MOD-02 instrument are specifically designed to serve this population and differentiate the Career Compass healthcare coaching pathway)

**Military transition overlay:** For all domain profiles, note that active security clearance is a significant market differentiator in the CSRA that does not appear in the adjacency matrix data. A client in any domain who holds a TS/SCI clearance should have clearance maintenance factored into their sector targeting — the DoD contractor corridor creates a clearance premium across every domain listed above.
