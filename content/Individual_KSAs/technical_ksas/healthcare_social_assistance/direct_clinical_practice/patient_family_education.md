ksa_id: patient_family_education
label: Patient & Family Education
category: Technical
description: |
  Covers the assessment of patient and family learning needs, design and delivery
  of individualized health education using plain-language and teach-back
  methodologies, evaluation of demonstrated comprehension, and documentation of
  education sessions and learning outcomes. Grounded in Joint Commission patient
  education standards, AHRQ health literacy frameworks, and QSEN
  Patient-Centered Care competencies.

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: "Under direct supervision, delivers assigned standardized patient
      education materials on a designated topic using plain-language explanations
      at approximately a sixth-grade reading level, and documents the teaching session
      in the patient record using the facility's education documentation form."
  - level: Basic
    indicator: "Assesses the patient's individual learning needs, health literacy
      level, and preferred learning modality before beginning an education session,
      selects teaching materials that match the assessed literacy level and primary
      language, uses the teach-back method to elicit a return demonstration or verbal
      restatement of key information, and documents the patient's demonstrated
      comprehension level and any content areas requiring follow-up teaching."
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: "Designs and delivers an individualized education plan for patients
      with multiple concurrent learning barriers — including low health literacy,
      non-English primary language, cognitive impairment, or sensory limitations —
      coordinates professional interpreter services or adaptive instructional materials
      as required, conducts a structured discharge readiness assessment with the patient
      and family, and documents a complete discharge education summary including
      teach-back verification for each key content domain."
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: "Identifies recurring gaps in the unit's patient education materials
      by reviewing documentation and readmission data, collaborates with the
      multidisciplinary team to develop or revise teaching resources for the unit's
      most common diagnoses, delivers structured training to nursing staff in
      teach-back methodology and evidence-based health literacy universal precautions,
      and evaluates the effectiveness of unit education interventions using available
      comprehension and readmission metrics."
  - level: Expert
    indicator: "Leads the design and ongoing evaluation of the unit or department's
      patient education program in alignment with Joint Commission PC.02.03.01
      standards, conducts structured audits of education documentation for completeness
      and teach-back verification compliance, establishes measurable patient education
      outcomes tied to defined readmission reduction and self-management targets,
      and serves as the department's clinical authority for health literacy policy
      and practice standards."
