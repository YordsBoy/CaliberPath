label: Infection Control & Sterile Technique
category: Technical
description: |
  Covers prevention and control of healthcare-associated infections through
  hand hygiene, standard and transmission-based precautions, selection and use
  of personal protective equipment, and aseptic and sterile technique for
  invasive clinical procedures. Grounded in CDC HICPAC infection prevention
  guidelines, APIC practice standards, and OSHA Bloodborne Pathogens Standard
  (29 CFR 1910.1030).

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: "Performs hand hygiene using the five-moment hand hygiene protocol
      (before patient contact, before an aseptic task, after body fluid exposure risk,
      after patient contact, and after contact with patient surroundings) before, during,
      and after each patient encounter; dons and doffs personal protective equipment in
      the designated sequence for standard precautions; and disposes of sharps
      immediately after each use in the nearest puncture-resistant disposal container."
  - level: Basic
    indicator: "Implements contact, droplet, or airborne transmission-based precautions
      by selecting and donning the correct PPE combination for the patient's isolation
      category upon entering the room; sets up and maintains an aseptic field for a
      basic invasive procedure — including urinary catheter insertion or peripheral
      venous access placement — using sterile technique from field preparation through
      completion; and reports observed PPE compliance deviations by peers to the charge
      nurse within the shift."
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: "Applies evidence-based bundle protocols for high-risk infection
      prevention procedures — including central venous catheter maintenance, urinary
      catheter management, and ventilator care — per facility policy; completes a
      self-audit of own infection control practice using the unit's structured
      observation checklist at a designated interval; and documents infection
      prevention-related assessments and all isolation-specific interventions in
      the patient record."
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: "Conducts structured peer-to-peer hand hygiene observation audits using
      the facility's standardized observation tool, compiles and presents audit results
      to the unit's infection prevention committee with trend analysis, identifies
      environmental and workflow factors contributing to infection control lapses on
      the unit, and develops targeted intervention proposals in collaboration with
      the facility's infection preventionist."
  - level: Expert
    indicator: "Leads the unit's infection prevention and control program in alignment
      with CDC HICPAC guidelines and Joint Commission NPSG 07.01.01 standards, designs
      and delivers annual infection control competency verification training for all
      nursing staff, evaluates the unit's healthcare-associated infection rates against
      NHSN benchmark data and presents findings to the patient safety committee, and
      coordinates with the hospital epidemiologist to activate and manage unit-level
      outbreak response protocols."
