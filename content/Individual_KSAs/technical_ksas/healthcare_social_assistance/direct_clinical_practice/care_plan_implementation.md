ksa_id: care_plan_implementation
label: Care Plan Development & Implementation
category: Technical
description: |
  Covers the development, implementation, and ongoing evaluation of individualized
  nursing care plans, including formulation of nursing diagnoses, patient-centered
  goal-setting, translation of provider orders into coordinated nursing interventions,
  and structured care plan communication during shift transitions. Grounded in ANA
  Nursing Standards of Practice and QSEN Patient-Centered Care competencies.

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: "Under direct supervision, completes all scheduled interventions
      specified in the assigned patient's care plan, documents each completed
      intervention in the patient record, and reports to the supervising nurse when
      an intervention cannot be completed as ordered, stating the specific reason
      for non-completion."
  - level: Basic
    indicator: "Implements an individualized care plan for two to three assigned
      patients, sequences competing care demands using a priority framework based
      on acuity and patient safety risk, adjusts intervention timing in response
      to acute patient condition changes, and documents each intervention alongside
      the patient's response in the care plan record."
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: "Formulates nursing diagnoses and patient-centered outcome goals for
      a full patient assignment using a standardized nursing diagnosis taxonomy,
      updates care plans in real time when reassessment data indicates a change in
      the patient's status or priorities, presents care plan updates using a
      structured handoff format at shift change, and incorporates documented patient
      and family preferences into stated care goals."
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: "Chairs or actively coordinates multidisciplinary care plan review
      conferences for complex patients with multiple co-morbidities or conflicting
      care priorities, reconciles discrepancies between provider orders and nursing
      care plan goals with documented clinical rationale, identifies patients at
      elevated risk for unplanned care deviations based on assessment trends, and
      provides structured care plan development coaching to newer nursing staff."
  - level: Expert
    indicator: "Evaluates the quality and clinical accuracy of care plan documentation
      across the unit through structured audits aligned with CMS §482.23 standards,
      leads the development of standardized evidence-based care plan templates for
      the unit's highest-volume diagnoses, identifies systemic gaps between documented
      care plans and observed nursing interventions through direct practice observation,
      and designs process improvement initiatives to close those gaps with measurable
      outcome targets."
