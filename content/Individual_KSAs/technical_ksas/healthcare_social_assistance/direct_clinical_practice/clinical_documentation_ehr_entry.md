label: Clinical Documentation & EHR Entry
category: Technical
description: |
  Covers accurate, timely, and legally defensible nursing documentation within
  electronic health record systems, including narrative nursing notes, structured
  data entry, error correction procedures, and compliance with CMS medical record
  standards and ONC interoperability requirements. Addresses documentation as
  both a clinical communication tool and a legal record of care delivery.

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: "Under direct supervision, locates and enters assigned documentation
      fields in the facility's electronic health record system — including vital signs,
      intake and output totals, and care activity records — and submits completed entries
      within the timeframe specified by unit policy."
  - level: Basic
    indicator: "Independently documents assessment findings, nursing interventions,
      and patient responses in the electronic health record using factually accurate
      and chronologically sequenced entries, applies facility-approved documentation
      templates for assigned note types, and refrains from prohibited documentation
      practices including advance charting, copy-forward use without clinical
      verification, and retroactive entry without a late-entry notation."
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: "Produces nursing documentation entries that capture the complete
      clinical reasoning sequence — assessment finding, nursing response, and patient
      outcome — in legally defensible language, corrects documentation errors using
      the facility's designated correction procedure (single line through error, initials,
      date, and correction entry), and reviews own documentation against the unit's
      completeness checklist before the end of each shift."
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: "Identifies documentation gaps or factual inconsistencies in the medical
      record that affect diagnostic coding accuracy or care continuity across shifts,
      communicates findings to the charge nurse or clinical informatics team with
      specific record reference citations, participates in structured documentation
      audits for the unit, and provides direct documentation coaching to orientees
      during their first 90 days of practice."
  - level: Expert
    indicator: "Develops or revises unit-specific documentation standards and electronic
      health record templates in collaboration with the clinical informatics team, leads
      staff education on documentation accuracy, legal defensibility, and CMS §482.24
      compliance, evaluates the unit's documentation practices against CMS §482.24
      standards through structured chart reviews, and serves as the unit's primary
      liaison for electronic health record optimization and workflow redesign initiatives."
