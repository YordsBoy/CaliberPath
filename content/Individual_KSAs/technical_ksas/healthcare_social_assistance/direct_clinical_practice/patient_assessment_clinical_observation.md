ksa_id: patient_assessment_clinical_observation
label: Patient Assessment & Clinical Observation
category: Technical
description: |
  Covers the systematic collection and documentation of patient health data through
  physical, psychosocial, and functional assessment techniques, including the
  interpretation of observation findings to inform nursing diagnosis and care
  prioritization. Encompasses both head-to-toe and focused assessments across
  acuity levels in direct care settings.

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: "Under direct supervision, completes a systematic head-to-toe physical
      assessment using a standardized assessment format, records vital signs and
      observable findings in the assigned section of the patient record, and reports
      completion status to the supervising nurse within the required timeframe."
  - level: Basic
    indicator: "Independently performs an initial head-to-toe assessment within the
      first hour of a shift, records abnormal findings (including abnormal breath sounds,
      changes in skin color or temperature, and altered orientation) in the patient
      record with descriptive clinical language, and reports each deviation from the
      documented baseline to the supervising nurse or provider before the end of the
      assessment sequence."
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: "Conducts and documents both focused and comprehensive assessments
      appropriate to the patient's clinical trajectory, distinguishes acute changes from
      chronic baseline findings through direct comparison with prior documentation,
      adjusts the frequency and depth of reassessment based on acuity indicators
      (including pain scores, respiratory pattern, and hemodynamic trends), and
      communicates assessment findings to the provider and care team using SBAR format."
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: "Leads bedside assessment rounds for a patient caseload of four to six
      patients, synthesizes assessment findings with available diagnostic and laboratory
      data to formulate nursing diagnoses and revise care priorities in real time when
      assessment data indicates acute change, and provides structured assessment coaching
      to orientees and newer staff using direct observation and immediate corrective
      feedback."
  - level: Expert
    indicator: "Establishes unit-wide assessment standards aligned with evidence-based
      clinical practice guidelines, evaluates the quality and completeness of nursing
      assessment documentation across the unit through structured chart audits, identifies
      recurring assessment practice gaps by category and staff tenure level, and designs
      targeted competency remediation interventions to close identified gaps."
