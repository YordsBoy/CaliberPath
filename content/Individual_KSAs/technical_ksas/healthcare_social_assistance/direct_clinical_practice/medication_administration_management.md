label: Medication Administration & Management
category: Technical
description: |
  Covers the safe preparation, verification, administration, and monitoring of
  medications across common routes and drug classes, including application of
  the rights of medication administration, high-alert medication protocols,
  adverse effect monitoring, and medication error identification and reporting.
  Grounded in medication safety guidelines and applicable nursing scope of
  practice standards.

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: "Under direct supervision, verifies the five rights of medication
      administration (right patient using two identifiers, right medication, right dose,
      right route, right time) before each administration event, and documents each
      completed administration in the medication administration record within the
      timeframe required by unit policy."
  - level: Basic
    indicator: "Independently administers scheduled and PRN medications via oral,
      subcutaneous, intramuscular, and IV piggyback routes, performs the required
      pre-administration assessments for parameter-dependent medications (including
      blood pressure verification before antihypertensives and blood glucose confirmation
      before insulin), withholds medications when assessment parameters fall outside
      prescribed limits, and notifies the provider of each hold with documented clinical
      rationale."
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: "Manages medication administration for a full patient assignment including
      high-alert medications (anticoagulants, concentrated electrolytes, and insulin
      infusions) using the facility's high-alert medication protocols, performs
      independent double-checks with a second licensed nurse per policy before
      administration of designated high-alert agents, monitors patients for therapeutic
      response and adverse reactions at required post-administration intervals, and
      documents assessment findings in direct temporal relationship to each
      administration event."
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: "Identifies medication errors and near-misses through real-time
      administration review, files reports through the facility's safety event reporting
      system with complete documentation of contributing factors, analyzes aggregate
      medication event data for the assigned unit to identify recurring error patterns,
      and delivers targeted education to colleagues on evidence-based high-alert
      medication safety practices specific to the identified patterns."
  - level: Expert
    indicator: "Leads the development or revision of unit-specific high-alert medication
      administration protocols in collaboration with pharmacy and medical staff leadership,
      designs and delivers structured medication safety competency programs for nursing
      staff, serves as the unit's subject matter authority for pharmacy-nursing interface
      issues, and evaluates unit-level compliance with high-alert medication policies
      through quarterly structured audits with findings reported to the patient safety
      committee."
