label: Early Deterioration Recognition & Escalation
category: Technical
description: |
  Covers the recognition of early clinical deterioration through systematic
  interpretation of physiological trend data, structured communication of clinical
  concern to the care team, and activation of rapid response and escalation
  protocols. Encompasses the application of standardized early warning scoring,
  SBAR communication, and facility rapid response team protocols to prevent
  progression to acute clinical decompensation.

proficiency_levels:
  - level: Awareness
    indicator: "Reports to the supervising nurse within the required timeframe when
      a patient's measured vital signs cross the facility's defined early warning
      threshold parameters, or when a patient directly communicates a subjective
      complaint of acute onset discomfort, shortness of breath, or change in
      functional status."
  - level: Basic
    indicator: "Calculates an early warning deterioration score using the facility's
      approved scoring tool at each scheduled assessment interval, communicates a
      clinical concern about an observed change in a patient's condition to the provider
      using SBAR format — specifying situation, background, assessment, and explicit
      recommendation — and documents the time of communication and the provider's
      response in the patient record."
  - level: Intermediate
    indicator: "Recognizes a pattern of early deterioration indicators — including
      new or increasing oxygen requirement, progressive tachycardia, decreasing urine
      output below 0.5 mL/kg/hour, or acute change in level of alertness — in the
      absence of a single alarming vital sign value; activates the facility's rapid
      response or critical care outreach team per protocol without waiting for provider
      acknowledgment; remains at the bedside to provide sequential assessment data
      to the responding team; and documents the chronological sequence of clinical
      changes, activation time, team arrival, and all interventions in the patient
      record."
  - level: Advanced
    indicator: "Facilitates structured debriefs after rapid response activations on
      the unit to identify the earliest clinical indicators present in the pre-activation
      period and determine whether earlier recognition was actionable, applies aggregate
      rapid response activation data to identify patient populations and clinical
      presentations with disproportionate deterioration frequency on the unit, and
      delivers structured coaching to junior nurses in SBAR escalation and early warning
      pattern interpretation using de-identified case examples."
  - level: Expert
    indicator: "Leads the unit's clinical deterioration recognition program in
      alignment with Joint Commission NPSG 16.01.01, evaluates the sensitivity and
      specificity of the facility's early warning scoring tool against actual rapid
      response activation outcomes through structured data analysis, prepares
      evidence-based recommendations for protocol modification to the patient safety
      committee, and collaborates with the intensivist team and clinical informatics
      staff to optimize deterioration alert criteria and escalation workflow within
      the electronic health record."
