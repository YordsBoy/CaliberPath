Gig & Platform Economy
======================

Freelance and on-demand platforms generated an estimated **$455 B** worldwide in 2025, yet workers face algorithmic opacity, safety risks, and fragmented compliance duties. Studies link algorithmic management to reduced autonomy and well-being, while Human Rights Watch flags deactivation without recourse as a top concern. TransUnion’s 2025 survey shows 54 % of gig workers would switch apps over fraud or safety incidents. Diversifying across multiple apps boosts earnings but also raises cognitive load and hidden costs. Tax authorities are tightening reporting rules—IRS gig guidance and OECD DAC7—forcing independent workers to upskill in finance compliance.  

This sector’s **11 KSAs** empower platform workers to master reputation, compliance, algorithmic literacy, multi-app strategy, and growth marketing while safeguarding safety and earnings.

## Labor Market Context
*Populated by T-README-02 | Data source: BLS OES + O\*NET + sector workforce studies*

**BLS Total Employment (current):** BLS Contingent Worker Supplement (2023): approximately 15.5 million independent contractors in the U.S., representing ~9.9% of the workforce. Gig platform-specific workers (Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, TaskRabbit, Upwork, Fiverr, etc.): estimated 59–73 million Americans participate in some form of gig work annually (Gallup 2023; McKinsey Global Institute). Full-time platform gig workers: estimated 10–15 million. No dedicated BLS SOC exists for gig platform workers; activities span dozens of occupational categories. Freelance market size: $455 billion globally (2025, Statista/Upwork).

**BLS 10-Year Projected Growth (% / absolute jobs):** BLS projects self-employed workers to grow +2.2% 2024–2034 (slightly slower than wage and salary employment at +3.1%). Gig platform work: growth is structural, not captured by traditional employment projections. IRS gig economy reporting (Form 1099-K threshold lowered) is expanding formal reporting. DAC7 (OECD) cross-border digital platform reporting: increasing compliance obligations for platform operators. Gig economy is projected to account for 50%+ of U.S. workforce on some basis by 2027 (Freelancers Union/Upwork). Regulatory environment (worker classification rules) remains the primary uncertainty.

**Median Annual Wage:** Highly variable and not separately reported by BLS. Proxy estimates: median gig worker annual earnings approximately $36,000–$45,000 (BLS CWS 2023; Gallup 2023 median estimate). Full-time freelancers on Upwork/Fiverr platforms: median $50,000–$65,000 in skilled categories (software, design, writing). Ride-share/delivery workers: $25,000–$38,000 annually (after platform fees and expenses, per TransUnion/Gridwise 2024 data). Professional freelancers (consulting, legal, tech): $75,000–$150,000+.

**Emerging / watch_2030 Roles:** Algorithmic Management Literacy Coach, Platform Dispute Resolution Specialist, Digital Marketing & Lead Generation Specialist (gig-to-direct client pipeline)

Augusta's military-adjacent population has historically high rates of gig work as supplemental income. Estimated 8,000–15,000 Augusta MSA residents participate in platform gig work to some degree. No formal BLS MSA-level estimate available for gig worker population.
